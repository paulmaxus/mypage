As a researcher, I am interested in the relationships between things in the world. "Is attention attracted more by color or by shape?" could be one of the questions I am asking.

As most of mathematics, statistics is abstract and therefore hard to grasp. I always found it easiest to understand abstract things when analogies are made or when visualizations are given. This is what I dedicate this section to.

  • Demonstration of linear regression
  • What is a Latin square design?

These mini apps were coded in Shiny by R and are accessible through R servers. Shiny applications are a great way of demonstrating statistics interactively. To upload your application to R, use the following code:

library(rsconnect)
# move to the top folder that contains
# your application and upload 
rsconnect::deployApp('yourApp')
