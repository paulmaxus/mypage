Speech, Brain & Technology

Max Paulus

My current research at the department of Speech, Hearing and Phonetic Sciences at the University College London is about the perceptual learning of degraded speech. A speech signal can be degraded due to the characteristics of the source, such as a speaker with an unfamiliar accent. Another possibility is that the listener has a hearing impairment which might have been compensated by technology such as hearing aids or cochlear implants.

Curriculum Vitae

From 2010-2014, I studied Computational Linguistics (B.Sc) at Saarland University in Germany. I did an exchange with the University of Technology in Sydney to study User interfaces, Databases and Web Systems. I stayed at Saarland University for my Master degree (2014-2017) in Language Science and Technology. I focused on the Neuroscience of Language and did my final project at the Center of Mind and Brain Sciences in Rovereto, Italy.
